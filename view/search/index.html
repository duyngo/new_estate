Area guides in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia
Kuala Lumpur is the capital of Malaysia which is also one of the most vibrant and developed cities of South East Asia region after Singapore. With an estimated population of 1.67million (Human Development Index 2014), the 3 main races are Malay, Chinese and Indian as well as a significant number of expatriates and foreign workers. Kuala Lumpur is the fastest growing metropolitan in the country with young and aggressive work force aims to achieve 2020 vision to become a high income and fully industrialized nation. Currently, high-rise skyscrapers, world-class hotels, ritzy shopping malls that offer local & international cuisine can be sighted as you roam on the streets. Not to forget the upcoming financial district, Tun Razak Exchange which is located just steps away from Bukit Bintang. As the capital of Malaysia, Kuala Lumpur is famous for its multi-racial Malaysian cuisine which basically consists of cooking practice from three major ethnic groups, Malay, Chinese and Indian. The food prices are reasonably priced and food and beverages outlets can be sighted easily, for example Mamak where most of them operate for 24 hours. Islam is the official religion of the federation. Having said that, freedom of religion is practised under Malaysia constitution. All ethnic groups are allowed to practice their own religion and as a result, the rich religion practice and beliefs embodied in daily life, cultural, and buildings. Mosques, Chinese and Hindu temples as well as churches can be spotted with their distinctive designs.
